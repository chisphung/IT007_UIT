The two plans illustrate changes that have been made to the layout of a school library over a period of five years.

Overall, the school library underwent several significant modifications. The most notable changes include the expansion of study spaces, the repurposing of existing rooms, and the introduction of facilities dedicated to technology.

Looking at the center of the map, the central studying area, which previously contained four tables, has been expanded to include six tables and transformed into a private studying space. On the left side of the library, the former classroom has been converted into a recording studio. Furthermore, the single borrowing and returning desk has been separated into two distinct areas—one for borrowing and one for returning—adding an extra room to this western wing.

In the southern section of the library, the bookshelves that were present five years ago have been replaced by desks for students' laptops. Similarly, on the eastern side of the plan, the previous computer desks have been updated to accommodate students' personal laptops. Finally, while the library office in the southeast corner has been converted into a conference room, the bookshelves in the northern section have remained completely unchanged.
