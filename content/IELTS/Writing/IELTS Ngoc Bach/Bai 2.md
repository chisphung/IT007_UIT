The provided floor plans illustrate the layouts and features of two different student accommodation options—a single room and a double room—at an Australian university.

Overall, the two-person room is significantly wider and more expensive than the single room. While both rooms share basic amenities such as a bathroom and kitchenette, the double room offers extra furniture to accommodate a second student, alongside additional seating.

Regarding dimensions and price, both rooms share a length of 6 metres, but the double room is wider (4 metres compared to 2.5 metres). Consequently, the double room costs $350 per week, which is $150 more than the single room. In terms of shared features, both rooms have an entrance on the western wall, with a bathroom immediately to the north. South of the door, both feature a kitchenette equipped with a stove, sink, and microwave. However, these appliances are arranged in a horizontal row in the single room, whereas they are stacked vertically along the wall in the double room.

Looking at the living and sleeping areas, the single room features one bed in the northeast corner, a study desk near the window, and a wall-mounted television on the southern wall. In contrast, the double room fits two beds, separated by a central partition made of a cupboard and bookshelves. It also provides two study desks—one by the window and one replacing the single room's television spot. Finally, the double room includes a small table and chairs in the southwest corner, situated opposite a wall-mounted television.
