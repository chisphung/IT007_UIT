The bar charts illustrate the top three producers in four different dairy products, along with their production volumes (in metric tons) in the year 2012.

Overall, five different countries and one region are featured in the charts, including the European Union, India, the US, Brazil, China, and New Zealand. While the EU recorded the largest figures in the Milk and Cheese categories, the US had the lowest production among the top three for milk and butter. Furthermore, milk production held the largest quantity over the three other types of dairy products.

Regarding the most heavily produced items, the EU dominated both milk and cheese categories. It was the largest producer of milk, generating a substantial 142.970 metric tons (MT), followed by India and the US with 127,000 MT and 90,038 MT, respectively. Similarly, the EU was the world's top producer of cheese, delivering 7,120 MT. The US was the second-largest cheese producer at 4,925 MT, while Brazil accounted for a much smaller fraction, providing merely 700 MT.

Conversely, butter and milk powder were produced in significantly smaller quantities. Butter production was led by India with 7,500 MT, which as more than triple the EU's output of 2,040 MT. The US contributed the least among the top three for this categories, at 810 MT. Finally, milk powder was the least produced product overall, driven largely by China (1,200 MT) and New Zealand (1,110 MT), with the EU acting as the third-largest notable contributor at 770 MT.
