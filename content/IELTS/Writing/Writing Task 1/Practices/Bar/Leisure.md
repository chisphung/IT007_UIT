The bar chart illustrates the number of hours spent by men and women in five different employment statuses (full-time employed, part-time employed, unemployed, retired and housewives) in the period from 1998 to 1999.

Overall, people who were unemployed or retired recorded a substantial amount of time spending on leisure activities, which was higher than the three other kinds of employment statuses. While the figures for part-time employees and housewives were only recorded for female workers, full-time employees were consistently the group spending the least leisure time. Furthermore, in all categories where both genders were presented, men enjoyed more leisure hours than women.

Regarding the demographics with the largest amount of leisure time, unemployed individuals spent roughly 84 hours and nearly 78 hours a week  for males and females, respectively.  These figures were followed by those for retired workers, using nearly 83 hours for men and 78 hours for women. 

Considering the three remaining kinds of working status, female housewives used exactly 50 hours a week for leisure time, followed by women part-time workers with 40 hours a week. Employed professionals spent the least time in leisure activities, recording roughly 45 hours and approximately 38 hours a week for males and females, respectively.
