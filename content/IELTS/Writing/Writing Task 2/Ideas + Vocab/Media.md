### **Essential Media, Advertising & Technology Vocabulary**

**Advertising & Consumerism**

- **Pervasive / Ubiquitous:** Found everywhere (a great way to describe modern advertising or smartphones).
    
- **Consumerism:** The preoccupation of society with the acquisition of consumer goods.
    
- **Targeted algorithms:** Computer programs that track user data to show them highly personalized advertisements.
    
- **Impulse buying:** Purchasing items on a whim, often driven by persuasive marketing rather than actual need.
    
- **Brand loyalty:** The tendency of some consumers to continue buying the same brand of goods rather than competing brands.
    

**Technology & Automation**

- **Automation:** The use of largely automatic equipment in a system of manufacturing or other production process.
    
- **Obsolete:** No longer produced or used; out of date (e.g., "Automation renders certain manual jobs obsolete").
    
- **Labor-saving devices:** Machines or software that reduce the amount of human effort required to do a task.
    
- **Digital divide:** The gap between demographics and regions that have access to modern information and communications technology, and those that don't.
    
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines.
    

**Media & Communication**

- **Information overload:** Exposure to or provision of too much information or data, leading to decision fatigue.
    
- **Echo chamber:** An environment where a person only encounters information or opinions that reflect and reinforce their own.
    
- **Digital footprint:** The information about a particular person that exists on the internet as a result of their online activity.

### **Essay Ideas & Arguments by Sub-Topic**

**1. The Increasing Influence of Advertising**

- **Why it's increasing:** We carry screens with us everywhere, meaning we are constantly exposed to marketing. Furthermore, social media platforms use complex algorithms to analyze our browsing habits, ensuring that the ads we see are hyper-personalized and highly persuasive.
    

**2. A Complete Ban on Advertising**

- **For a Ban:** It would drastically reduce consumer debt and improve societal mental health by stopping the constant promotion of unrealistic lifestyles and beauty standards.
    
- **Against a Ban (Counter-argument):** Advertising is the economic engine of the free internet. Without ad revenue, free services like social media, news websites, and search engines would have to charge high subscription fees. It also suffocates new businesses that rely on advertising to find their first customers.
    

**3. Consumers Becoming Less Influenced by Ads**

- **Reasons:** Modern consumers suffer from "ad fatigue" due to the sheer volume of daily marketing. Furthermore, the internet allows buyers to research products independently. People now trust peer-reviewed sites, unboxing videos, and verified customer reviews much more than corporate marketing campaigns.
    

**4. Advertising Only Encourages Buying Unneeded Items**

- **Agree:** The core strategy of many industries (like fast fashion or smartphone manufacturers) is "planned obsolescence"—using advertising to make consumers feel their perfectly functional older models are outdated and socially unacceptable.
    
- **Disagree:** Advertising also serves a vital educational purpose. Governments use it for public health campaigns (e.g., anti-smoking or vaccination ads). Companies use it to inform the public about genuinely useful, life-saving innovations like smart home security or water-purifying technology.
    

**5. The Internet as the Most Important Invention**

- **For the Internet:** It has completely democratized information, allowing anyone with a connection to access the sum of human knowledge. It has also removed geographical borders for trade, creating a truly globalized economy.
    
- **Against (Other Inventions):** While revolutionary, the internet is a secondary invention. It relies entirely on the prior invention of electricity. Historically, the wheel, the printing press, and antibiotics (penicillin) had a more fundamental impact on human survival and the initial development of civilization.
    

**6. Robots Taking Over Home and Work Tasks**

- **Positive Development:** In the home, labor-saving devices free up hours of time, allowing adults to focus on their children or side careers. At work, robots take over hazardous, physically grueling tasks (like mining or heavy assembly), protecting human health and increasing manufacturing efficiency.
    
- **Negative Development:** It leads to massive job displacement for blue-collar workers. It can also lead to a loss of the "human touch" in service industries, making society feel more sterile and isolated.
    

**7. Unequal Benefits of Communications Technology (The Digital Divide)**

- **The Divide:** While urban populations in wealthy nations enjoy fiber-optic internet and 5G, rural areas and developing nations often lack basic connectivity.
    
- **The Consequences:** This creates a severe educational and economic disadvantage. Furthermore, there is a generational divide; elderly people often lack digital literacy, leaving them isolated as essential services (like banking and government forms) move exclusively online.
    

**8. Banning Mobile Phones in Public Places**

- **For a Ban:** Phones cause massive public disruptions (loud conversations in quiet train carriages or cinemas) and pose serious privacy risks, as anyone can be recorded or photographed without consent in gyms or swimming pools.
    
- **Against a Ban:** Phones are essential safety devices. In an emergency, immediate access to a phone can save a life. Additionally, enforcing a blanket ban in all public spaces is practically impossible and a severe infringement on personal liberty.
