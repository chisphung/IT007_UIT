### **Essential Sports & Leisure Vocabulary**

**Athletics & Funding**

- **Elite athletes:** Sports competitors at the highest national or international level.
    
- **Grassroots sports:** Sports programs and initiatives aimed at the general public and children, rather than professionals.
    
- **State-of-the-art facilities:** The most recent, advanced, and highly equipped training centers or stadiums.
    
- **National prestige:** The respect and admiration a country receives on the global stage.
    
- **White elephant:** A possession that is useless or troublesome, especially one that is expensive to maintain or difficult to dispose of (often used to describe abandoned Olympic stadiums).
    

**Risk & Regulation**

- **Extreme sports:** Activities perceived as involving a high degree of risk (e.g., skydiving, base jumping).
    
- **Adrenaline junkie:** A person with a compulsive desire for excitement and adventure.
    
- **Assumption of risk:** A legal and philosophical concept where an individual voluntarily takes on the dangers associated with an activity.
    
- **Nanny state:** A government that interferes too much with personal choice (useful here just as in the health topics).
    

**Entertainment & Media**

- **Palpable atmosphere:** A feeling or mood in a room (like at a concert) that is so intense it seems almost tangible.
    
- **Immersive experience:** An activity that completely absorbs your attention and senses.
    
- **High-definition broadcasting:** Modern TV and internet streams that offer crystal-clear visual and audio quality.
    
- **Communal experience:** An event shared and enjoyed together by a group of people.

### **Essay Ideas & Arguments by Sub-Topic**

**1. Funding Elite Competitors vs. Grassroots Children's Sports**

- **For Elite Competitors:** Many countries spend heavily to prepare athletes for major events like the Olympics or the World Cup. Winning medals boosts national prestige and unites the country. Furthermore, successful elite athletes become powerful role models, inspiring the next generation to become active.
    
- **For Children's Sports:** It is argued that it would be better to spend this money encouraging children to take up sports. Investing in grassroots programs acts as a preventative healthcare measure. Building local sports facilities and subsidizing youth leagues fights the youth obesity epidemic and teaches children teamwork and discipline, providing a much higher long-term return on investment for society as a whole.
    

**2. Hosting International Sports Events (e.g., The Olympics)**

- **A Waste of Money:** Hosting major international events costs an astronomical amount of money. Opponents argue this is wasted money that could be spent on critical public services like healthcare or education. Furthermore, cities often go into massive debt building specialized stadiums that become decaying "white elephants" once the few weeks of competition are over.
    
- **Not a Waste (Counter-argument):** Others believe the opposite, noting that hosting forces a city to rapidly upgrade its public infrastructure (roads, transport, airports). It also acts as a massive global advertisement, triggering a long-term tourism boom and creating thousands of temporary jobs.
    

**3. Banning Dangerous Sports vs. Freedom to Choose**

- **For a Ban:** Some people think governments should ban dangerous sports. When thrill-seekers inevitably get injured, their rescue and subsequent medical treatments place an unfair financial burden on taxpayer-funded public healthcare systems.
    
- **For Freedom of Choice:** Others believe everyone should have the freedom to choose their sporting activities. Banning these sports is a severe infringement on personal autonomy. Instead of banning them, governments should enforce strict safety regulations and require participants to carry private medical insurance.
    

**4. Live Performances vs. Watching on TV/Computers**

- **Watching at Home is Better:** Some argue there is no longer a need to go out for live performances because watching at home is superior. Modern technology provides high-definition screens, perfect audio, and multiple camera angles that offer a better view than a cheap concert ticket. It is also significantly more accessible and cost-effective.
    
- **Live is Still Necessary (Counter-argument):** A screen can never replicate the palpable atmosphere and raw energy of a live show. Attending a concert or play is a deeply communal experience that allows fans to connect with each other and directly support the artists financially.
