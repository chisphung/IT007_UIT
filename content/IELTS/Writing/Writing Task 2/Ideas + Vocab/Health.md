### **Essential Health & Diet Vocabulary**

**Nutrition & Lifestyle**

- **Sedentary lifestyle:** A way of life that involves little physical activity and a lot of sitting.
    
- **Processed foods:** Foods that have been altered during preparation, often containing artificial preservatives, high sodium, and refined sugar.
    
- **Empty calories:** Food that supplies energy but has little or no nutritional value (e.g., soda, candy).
    
- **Nutritional awareness:** Knowledge about what constitutes a healthy diet and the contents of the food one consumes.
    
- **Food deserts:** Urban or rural areas where it is difficult to buy affordable or good-quality fresh food.
    

**Public Health & Diseases**

- **Obesity epidemic:** The rapid increase in the proportion of overweight people globally.
    
- **Cardiovascular diseases:** Conditions affecting the heart or blood vessels (often linked to poor diet).
    
- **Chronic conditions:** Illnesses that persist over a long period (e.g., Type 2 diabetes).
    
- **Preventative medicine:** Healthcare practices aimed at preventing diseases rather than curing them (e.g., promoting exercise).
    

**Government Policy & Society**

- **Nanny state:** A government perceived as overprotective or interfering too much in personal choice (a highly effective term for IELTS essays).
    
- **Paternalistic:** A system where the authority restricts the freedom and responsibilities of its subordinates (citizens) supposedly in their best interest.
    
- **Sugar tax / Sin tax:** A state-imposed tax on products deemed harmful to society to discourage consumption.
    
- **Individual autonomy:** The right of a person to make their own choices about their life and body.

### **Essay Ideas & Arguments by Sub-Topic**

**1. Banning Unhealthy Food and Drinks**

- **For a Ban:** Banning scientifically proven toxic products (like foods with high trans-fats) acts as a preventative measure to alleviate the massive financial strain on public healthcare systems. It protects vulnerable populations, especially children who are highly susceptible to junk food marketing.
    
- **Against a Ban:** A total ban is an extreme infringement on individual autonomy; it creates a "nanny state" where the government treats adults like children. Furthermore, it could devastate the economy by causing massive job losses in the food and beverage industry. A better alternative is heavy taxation combined with education.
    

**2. Reasons for Unhealthy Habits & Solutions (Obesity)**

- **Reasons:** The modern work culture has created a time-poor society where working parents rely on convenient, highly processed fast food rather than cooking fresh meals. Additionally, junk food is often artificially cheap to produce, while organic produce is expensive. Finally, jobs and entertainment (screen time) have become increasingly sedentary.
    
- **Solutions:** Governments should heavily subsidize the agricultural sector to make fresh fruits and vegetables cheaper than processed alternatives. Schools must integrate mandatory nutritional cooking classes into the curriculum, and strict bans should be placed on advertising junk food during children's television programming.
    

**3. Are Diet and Exercise "Necessary" for a Long/Healthy Life?**

- **For Necessity:** Scientifically, regular cardiovascular exercise and a balanced diet directly prevent chronic illnesses like heart disease, diabetes, and stroke. They also fortify the immune system and delay cognitive decline in old age.
    
- **The Nuance/Counter-argument:** While highly beneficial, they are not strictly the _only_ necessities, nor do they guarantee a long life. Genetics play a massive role in life expectancy. Furthermore, mental health (low stress levels), environmental factors (breathing clean air, drinking unpolluted water), and access to modern healthcare are equally critical components of longevity.
    

**4. Government vs. Individual Responsibility for Diet**

- **For Government Responsibility:** Individuals cannot make healthy choices if their environment does not support it. The government has the power to regulate food monopolies, force companies to display clear nutritional labels, and eradicate "food deserts." Public health is a national security and economic issue, so the state must intervene.
    
- **For Individual Responsibility:** Ultimately, the state cannot monitor what citizens eat in their own homes. Adults must take accountability for their own self-discipline. Even with the best public education and cheap vegetables, individuals can still choose to eat poorly and avoid exercise.
    
- **Synthesis (Great for Conclusions):** The most effective approach is a partnership. The government should provide the optimal environment (affordable healthy food and education), but the final execution relies on individual willpower.
