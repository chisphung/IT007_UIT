**Some children these days spend most of their free time taking part in sport in a very serious way. These children do not take part in sport only for fun. Is this a positive or negative development?**

It is increasingly common today for children to dedicate the majority of their free time to rigorous, professional sports training rather than playing for fun. From my own perspective, this is large negative development. While it may build discipline, I believe that forcing youngsters to treat sports seriously can lead to psychological stress and severely disrupt their ability to balance their time between academics and leisure. 

The primary reason children spend their spare time attending professional sports training courses is a negative development is that they can be easily stressed because of the lack of recreational activities. Indeed, more and more parents wanting their offspring to become professional players, which frequently requires a massive amount of training time and playing hours. Since most of their time is dedicated in school, the only option is using children's free time attending the training courses. However, this action can inevitably lead to some detrimental effects, regarding to their mental health and the degradation in academic results. 

It is clear that some parents' goals is to provide a strong background in sports skills and professional experience; however, the overload of the two main activities in their life, including academic and sports activities, could decrease the overall performance in both sides.  For example, beside some hard training activities with their mentors, children also need to handle a massive amount of assignments. While the two duties require their personal free time to complete, their time cannot be allocated flawlessly to balance their activities. 

In conclusion, encouraging children to participate serious sports activities should be considered carefully. Forcing children to engage these activities can indirectly ruin their life because of stressful training and the degradation in time management skills.

### **Corrected Essay**

> It is increasingly common today for children to dedicate the majority of their free time to rigorous, professional sports training rather than playing for fun. From my perspective, this is a largely negative development. While it may build discipline, I believe that forcing youngsters to treat sports so seriously can lead to psychological stress and severely disrupt their ability to balance their time between academics and leisure.
> 
> The primary reason this trend is negative is that children can easily become stressed due to a lack of unstructured recreational activities. Indeed, an increasing number of parents want their children to become professional players, which frequently requires a massive amount of training time and playing hours. Since most of a child's day is dedicated to school, the only option is to use their free time to attend these training courses. However, this action inevitably leads to detrimental effects regarding their mental health and a decline in academic performance.
> 
> It is clear that some parents' goal is to provide a strong background in sports skills and professional experience; however, the overload of the two main activities in their lives, namely academics and sports, could decrease their overall performance in both areas. For example, besides undergoing rigorous training with their mentors, children also need to handle a massive amount of school assignments. Since both duties require their personal free time to complete, their time cannot be allocated flawlessly to balance these heavy demands.
> 
> In conclusion, the trend of encouraging children to participate in serious sports activities should be considered carefully. Forcing youngsters to engage in such intense regimens can indirectly harm their well-being due to stressful training environments and the deterioration of their time management skills.
