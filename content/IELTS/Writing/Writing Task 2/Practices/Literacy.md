
**Despite better access to education, a significant number of adults cannot read or write. In what ways are people disadvantaged without these skills? What could governments do about this problem?**

It is widely perceived that the ability to read and write is one of the most necessary skills. However, a large proportion of society, especially the adults, cannot write or read, leading to some specific disadvantages, regarding access to information. This essay will convey these drawbacks and propose practical solutions that governments could implement. 

The biggest detrimental effect that directly impacts the adults' lives is that they cannot interact with human knowledge written in text, which makes them easily manipulated by other sources of information. It is clear that newspapers, magazines and detailed documents are all described in text, making literacy one of the most vital skills. However, people who are unable to read cannot access this impactful information, which inevitably urges them to find other sources to comprehend. This consequently drives this group to an information trap, in which people only trust information around them rather than dive deeper into a problem in various perspectives. Furthermore, writing is an essential skill in expressing their emotion, but people without this literacy cannot show their opinions, which indirectly excludes some valuable ideas from a part of society.


In line with the development of technology, governments can approach individuals without the ability of reading or writing conveniently by digital devices. Indeed, providing a single application with the function of learning the alphabet and practicing people's reading and writing skills can promote the learning progress rapidly. However, some specific languages are extremely complex to understand and write. Some countries can solve this by simplifying their languages to encourage citizens to learn their languages. Take China as an example, while this country previously had only 10% of citizens with the ability to read and write, the government applied a regulation to simplify their language, reducing the complexity of text representation and grammar. Therefore, the literacy rate has increased rapidly over the years. 

In conclusion,  literacy is one of the most important factors in the way we approach information. The inability to use these essential skills could reduce the opportunity to approach insightful information and increase the potential of being manipulated. Governments should modernize their approach in teaching languages or simplify their languages to assist the adults in learning these skills.

> It is widely perceived that the ability to read and write is one of the most necessary skills in life. However, a large proportion of society, especially adults, lacks basic literacy, leading to significant disadvantages regarding access to information. This essay will discuss these drawbacks and propose practical solutions that governments could implement.
> 
> The most detrimental effect on **illiterate** adults is their inability to interact with written knowledge, which leaves them easily manipulated by alternative sources of information. Newspapers, magazines, and official documents are primarily text-based, making literacy a vital survival skill. Consequently, individuals who are unable to read cannot access reliable data, which inevitably forces them to rely on word-of-mouth. This drives this demographic into an information trap, where they only trust what they hear from those immediately around them rather than analyzing issues from various objective perspectives. Furthermore, writing is an essential tool for expressing emotions and opinions; without it, a significant portion of society is indirectly excluded from sharing valuable ideas.
> 
> To address this, governments can leverage technological developments to reach illiterate individuals through digital devices. Providing an accessible application designed to teach the alphabet and practice basic reading and writing can accelerate the learning process. However, some languages are notoriously complex to master. To counter this, countries can simplify their written languages to encourage citizens to learn them. Take China as an example: when the country previously had a literacy rate of only 10%, the government introduced regulations to simplify the language, reducing the complexity of its characters and grammar. As a result, the national literacy rate has increased dramatically over the years.
> 
> In conclusion, literacy is one of the most important factors in how we acquire knowledge. The inability to use these essential skills reduces opportunities to access insightful information and increases the risk of manipulation. Governments should modernize their educational methods or simplify their written languages to properly assist adults in acquiring these fundamental skills.
