From my own perspective, I partly agree with this statement, as I believe that teachers dominate a child’s intellectual advancement, while parents remain the most profound influence on their social development. 

The primary reason teachers have a great impact on intellectual development is their specialized expertise and structural learning environment they provide. Schools usually offer a rigorous curriculum designed to help students develop their cognitive abilities, and educators are trained in specialized pedagogical methods to explain complex terminologies or challenging concepts that challenge parents in teaching at home without engineering or technical background. For instance, a skilled teacher can guide a young student to deal with mathematics, logical lessons or even introductory coding that parents are not fully grasped. Consequently, the classrooms serve as a main engine for a child's academic and intellectual growth.

Therefore, despite social interactions that happen in schoolyards, parents provide a fundamental blueprint to children’s social intelligence. 

In conclusion, educators frequently provide children with knowledge with their professional expertise. They also contribute to children's social interaction behaviors but parents have already set a profound foundational manner that they should follow.
