### 1. Education & Learning

This topic focuses on teaching methods, educational priorities, and the learning environment.

- Whether it is better for adults to learn practical skills independently or with a teacher in a classroom.
    
- The debate over replacing traditional books with films, TV, video games, and computers in schools.
    
- The effectiveness of students studying in groups versus studying on their own.
    
- Whether a university education is the best path to success.
    
- The value of spending national funds on vocational training for practical work instead of university education.
    
- If secondary school children should be required to study international news.
    
- The benefits and problems associated with students moving to another country for their higher education.
    
- The disadvantages faced by adults who cannot read or write, and how governments can solve this.
    
- Whether teachers have more influence on a child's intellectual and social development than parents once the child starts school.
    

### 2. Environment, Animals & Space

These questions deal with conservation, resource management, and planetary exploration.

- The argument over whether zoos are cruel and should be closed, or if they are useful for protecting rare animals.
    
- Whether humanity should only protect animals that are useful to humans.
    
- The dangers and solutions regarding the rapid consumption of the world's natural resources, such as oil, forests, and fresh water.
    
- Whether the main benefit of international cooperation is environmental protection or business.
    
- If animal extinction is a natural process that people should not try to prevent.
    
- Funding research on how humans could live on other planets, such as Mars, due to future difficulties living on Earth.
    
- The causes and solutions for the declining numbers of plant and animal species.
    
- The reasons for a lack of waste recycling and how to encourage it.
    
- Whether pollution and environmental damage are unavoidable results of a country developing and becoming richer.
    

### 3. Work & Employment

This category covers career choices, workplace policies, and the job market.

- The problems caused by older people competing with younger people for the same jobs.
    
- The reliability of interviews as a job selection method compared to other procedures.
    
- The reasons why young people frequently change jobs or careers, and the advantages versus disadvantages.
    
- Whether a satisfying career is the most important element of a person's life.
    
- If governments should restrict extremely high salaries.
    
- The claim that employers should give staff at least four weeks' holiday per year to improve job performance.
    
- Laws that limit the number of hours an employee can work.
    

### 4. Government, Infrastructure & Society

This theme includes public spending, urban planning, and societal structures.

- Providing free, 24/7 public transport as the best way to solve city traffic congestion.
    
- Moving large companies and their employees to the countryside to solve traffic and housing problems.
    
- Investing more resources in young people to prepare a country for the future.
    
- Whether governments should spend public money supporting the arts or focus on health and education.
    
- Spending money on new public buildings rather than relocating or renovating existing ones.
    
- Prioritizing health care over other areas when spending taxpayer money.
    
- The importance of having large outdoor public spaces, such as squares and parks, in towns and cities.
    
- Producing a happier society by ensuring only a small difference exists between the earnings of the richest and poorest.
    

### 5. Health & Diet

These prompts revolve around lifestyle choices, nutrition, and public health policies.

- Whether shops should be banned from selling food or drink scientifically proven to be bad for health.
    
- The reasons for unhealthy eating habits leading to obesity, and the best ways to improve these habits.
    
- The debate over whether a good diet and regular exercise are necessary for a long and healthy life.
    
- If governments should do more to ensure citizens have a healthy diet, or if individuals must take their own responsibility.
    

### 6. Globalization, Culture & Travel

This topic addresses international relations, cultural shifts, and tourism.

- Whether charity organizations should prioritize global aid or focus on helping people in their own country.
    
- The debate on whether it is possible to be a "responsible tourist" who preserves local culture and environment.
    
- The causes and effects of more people traveling to other countries.
    
- Differences between countries becoming less evident due to shared films, fashion, brands, and TV channels.
    
- Whether working for a multinational organization is the best way to understand other cultures.
    
- The increasing similarity of places around the world compared to the past.
    
- The belief that traditional customs and behaviors are no longer relevant to modern life.
    

### 7. Media, Advertising & Technology

These questions look at the impact of technology, advertising, and the internet on daily life.

- The increasing influence of advertising on our lives.
    
- The argument that society would benefit from a complete ban on all forms of advertising.
    
- Reasons why consumers are now less influenced by advertising.
    
- The claim that the only purpose of advertising is to encourage people to buy more than they need.
    
- Whether the internet is the most important invention in human history.
    
- The shift toward tasks at home and work being done by robots.
    
- The unequal distribution of benefits from modern communications technology.
    
- Whether mobile phones should be banned in public places.
    

### 8. Crime & Law

This theme covers the justice system, punishment, and preventing criminal behavior.

- Using better education as a more effective solution to crime than prison.
    
- The reasons why children and teenagers are committing more crimes and how they should be punished.
    
- The problem of criminals committing more crimes after being released from prison, and potential solutions.
    

### 9. Family, Children & Youth

These prompts focus on child development, parenting roles, and family dynamics.

- Parents reading stories to children versus relying on books, movies, and TV.
    
- The advantages and disadvantages of parents moving abroad for work and taking their families.
    
- Children spending their free time taking sports very seriously instead of playing for fun.
    
- Requiring parents to take annual courses on parenting and child development.
    
- Fathers staying at home to look after children while mothers work full-time.
    
- Whether the family, or other influences like television and friends, has the most powerful effect on a child's development.
    
- Reasons why young people do not spend time doing outdoor activities in the natural environment.
    
- The value of researching family history and previous generations.
    

### 10. Sports & Leisure

This final category covers athletics, recreational activities, and entertainment.

- Spending national funds on preparing elite competitors for major sports events versus encouraging young children to take up sports.
    
- Whether it is a waste of money for a country to host international sports events like the Olympic Games.
    
- If governments should ban dangerous sports or allow individuals the freedom to choose.
    
- The debate over whether live performances are necessary now that we can watch them on TV or computers.
