- **An important old thing / kept in your family for a long time:** Focus on the physical condition of the book (yellowed pages, leather binding) and its emotional value to your parents and you.
    
- **Something you can’t live without (not a phone/PC):** Focus on how you use it daily as a diary or for moral guidance when you are stressed.
    
- **A book you found useful:** Focus on the specific life advice or traditional natural remedies written inside that helped you solve a problem.

### 2. The Universal Structure

No matter which of the four prompts you receive, use this 4-step structure to organize your 2 minutes:

1. **The Hook (15 seconds):** Introduce the item immediately and confidently.
    
    - _Example:_ "I’d like to talk about my most cherished possession, which is a leather-bound heirloom journal passed down from my late grandfather."
        
2. **The Origin (30 seconds):** Briefly explain where it came from.
    
    - _Example:_ "He gifted it to me on my 18th birthday, just before I moved away for university. He had been writing in it for over forty years."
        
3. **The Pivot (45 seconds):** **This is where you answer the specific cue card.** (See Step 3 below).
    
4. **The Feeling (30 seconds):** Conclude with the final bullet point (how you feel about it).
    
    - _Example:_ "Ultimately, it makes me feel deeply grounded. Whenever I read it, I feel a profound sense of comfort, almost as if he is still here guiding me."
        

**If the prompt is: An important old thing / family item**

> **Focus on age and preservation:** "Because it is over half a century old, the pages are incredibly fragile and yellowed. We keep it stored safely in a wooden box to protect it from humidity. It’s vital to my family because it is the only physical record we have of my grandfather's early life."

**If the prompt is: Something you can’t live without**

> **Focus on daily reliance:** "I genuinely cannot imagine my daily routine without it. I use it as an emotional anchor. Whenever I feel overwhelmed by work or life choices, I flip through his handwritten profound life lessons. It instantly calms my anxiety."

**If the prompt is: A book you found useful**

> **Focus on a specific problem solved:** "What makes this book so practical is the section on herbal remedies. Last winter, I had a terrible, persistent cough. Instead of taking standard medicine, I followed his meticulously handwritten recipe for a ginger and lotus tea. It worked wonders, proving just how useful this journal is."

**If the prompt is: An interesting traditional story**

> **Focus on folklore:** "The most captivating part of the journal isn't the advice, but a specific chapter where he documented local folklore. He wrote down a fascinating myth about the mountain village where he grew up, involving a spirit that protected the harvest. It reads like a beautiful fairytale."
