## Hometown 

1. Where do you come from?

- I come from Đồng Tháp, which is in the western part of Vietnam. It's well-known for its lotus flowers, which are also considered the symbol of the province. Whenever I tell my friends I'm from Đồng Tháp, they usually ask me if I'm from Tháp Mười — that place is famous for its beautiful lotus lakes and a wide variety of local dishes and specialty foods

1. Which town or city do you live in now?

	I’m living in Ho Chi Minh City, the largest city in Vietnam. It’s a busy and vibrant place with modern buildings, amazing street food, and nonstop traffic
2. Are there any things you don't like about your area? (What are they?)

	Two things I don’t really like about the city are air pollution and traffic congestion. The combination of poor air quality and heavy traffic can be quite unpleasant. Fortunately, I live in a more remote area of the city, so I don’t have to deal with these problems very often.

3. Do you think you will continue to live there for a long time? (Why? Why not?)

	Probably not. In the near future, I’ll graduate from my current university, and I’m planning to pursue a PhD degree. Because of that, I’ll most likely move to another country to continue my studies.”

4. What are some changes in the area recently?

	Recently, my area has changed quite a lot. Many new buildings and infrastructure projects are being developed, and I think it will become a much more modern city in the future

5. Do you live in a house or an apartment?

	I currently live in a dormitory at Vietnam National University. It’s considered one of the most modern student dormitories in Southeast Asia, but honestly, I think the facilities and services aren’t good enough to deserve that title.

6. What is your favourite room in your home? (Why?)

	I think many people would choose their bedroom, but my favourite room is the kitchen. Since I’m living in a dormitory now, the kitchen in my hometown is a place where I can cook and practise my cooking skills

7. What things make your home pleasant to live in? (Why?)

	My home is pleasant because it gives me a strong sense of safety and comfort. When I sleep in my bed at my hometown, I feel much more secure and relaxed. In contrast, living in a dormitory doesn’t always give me the same quality of sleep
	
8. Are the people in your neighbourhood nice and friendly?

	Honestly, I don’t interact with my neighbours very much. From what I’ve noticed, they can be quite noisy because they often play online games and talk loudly with their teammates. Sometimes they even play music while gaming, which creates a lot of noise

9. Do you know any of your neighbours?

	Yes, I know all of them, but interestingly, I haven’t actually talked to some of them at all. That’s mainly because I don’t spend much time in my room, which is just part of my personality

10. Do you know any famous people in your area?

	Yes, there’s a singer at my university who is quite well known among students. He’s very funny and creative. As far as I know, he has been performing since he was in high school, and I once talked with him. He shared some really interesting insights about music.
